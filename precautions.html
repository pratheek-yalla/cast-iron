Microstructural analysis of cast-iron specimens

  • Difficulties:

    1. The main challenge when preparing samples of cast iron is to retain the graphite in its original shape and size to ensure a correct representation of the cast iron's microstructures.

      insufficient polish
      correct polish

    2. There is always a possibility that the graphite cannot be completely retained, especially very large flakes or agglomerations of flakes. As a result, the graphite phase cannot always be retained or polished well.

    3. Insufficient removal of smeared matrix metal after grinding, can obscure the true shape and size of graphite, particularly in ferritic and austenitic cast irons. For these materials, a thorough diamond and final polish is very important.

      insufficient polish
      correct polish

    4. With a magnification of 100x, the graphite appear black. Higher magnifications are required to verify if the carbon is completely retained, as well polished graphite is grey.

  • Precautions:

    1. Keep the room and the work area clean.
    2. Don't touch etched and polished surface of the specimen.
    3. Don't touch the lances of eyepiece with dirty hand.
    4. Use only clean clothes to clean the lenses of eyepieces.
    5. Handle the microscope with gently.
    6. Cleaning must be done fast & carried out with cold water since cast irons tend to corrode. The sample must not be left in contact with water.
    7. Thorough rinsing with ethanol & fast drying with strong stream of warm air is recommended .If corrosion still occurs, clean & rinse with water free alcohol only.
    8. Switch off the microscope after the observation.
