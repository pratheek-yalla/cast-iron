Microstructural analysis of cast-iron specimens

  • Procedure

    1. Once the experiment is open click on 'Next' to proceed.
    2. Read about recommendation about cutting. Click on 'Next' to proceed.
    3. Read the details about mounting and proceed by clicking on 'Next'
    4. Read about grinding recommendation. Click on 'Start Grinding'.
    5. Click on 'Next' after grinding operation.
    6. Read the details about polishing. Click on 'Start Polishing'.
    7. Click on 'Next' after polishing operation.
    8. Go through the details of etching. Click on 'Start Etching'.
    9. Click on 'Next' after etching.
    10. Click on 'Microscope' to place the specimen.
    11. To watch the microstructure click on 'Watch the microstructure'.
    12. You can select the zoom by clicking on 100x and 200x.
    13. Answer the question below and click on 'Check'.
    14. If the correct you can print the image for further analysis.

  • Analysis:

    1. Line Method:
      • Draw a line of known length (L1) on the given microstructure. For example in following figure, assume that we are interested in finding the average size of the dispersed phase i.e. grey color phase.
      • Measure length of the line passes through the desired phase i.e. L11, L12 and L13.
      • Draw as many as possible lines and measures the length of at least 100 grains of desired phase.
      • Tabulate the results.
      • Then find the average size of dispersed phase, fraction of dispered phase and phase distribution.
    2. Point Count Method:
      • To decide whether the distribution of second phase is uniform or not.
      • Form a grid containing 20 points and overlap the grid on the microstructure as shown above. Indeed the points falling on the dispersed phase are drawn with black colour. Count the number of points that appear on the dispersed phase.
      • Find the phase fraction from the data.
      • From the data, one can guess SD of the data and concluded the distribution of the phase.
